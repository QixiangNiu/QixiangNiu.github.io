Qixiang Niu is currently a visiting Ph.D. student in the Department of Signal Theory and Communications at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (UC3M) (Leganés campus) in Spain. During his visit, his foreign supervisor is David Ramírez, and the visit is funded by Northwestern Polytechnical University. Meanwhile, he is also a Ph.D. candidate in the major of Information Perception and Recognition Technology at the School of Marine Science and Technology (SMST) of Northwestern Polytechnical University (NWPU), under the supervision of Qunfei Zhang and Wentao Shi.

He obtained his Bachelor of Engineering degree in Information Engineering from NWPU in 2019. In 2021, he earned his Master of Engineering degree in Signal Perception and Detection from NWPU. In the same year, he received NWPU’s approval to commence his Ph.D. studies early, and he began his doctoral studies in March.

His current research interests include integrated systems for underwater detection and communication (ISUDC), integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), underwater acoustic waveform design, direct-path suppression in underwater environments, multistatic target localization, data fusion, signal detection and recognition, and the Internet of Vehicles.
